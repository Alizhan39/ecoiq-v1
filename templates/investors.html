The Problem

No Unified ESG Intelligence Layer

Institutional investors, development banks, and sovereign funds lack a single, AI-powered source of truth for climate and governance scoring across emerging and frontier markets — particularly Central Asia, the Gulf, and UK industrial sectors.

Existing Platforms Are Too Expensive or Too Slow

Bloomberg ESG, MSCI, and Sustainalytics cost £50K–£500K/year per seat and cover large-cap equities only. Mid-market industrial companies — the real transition bottleneck — are entirely unscored.

02

The Solution

EcoIQ — Ethical Intelligence Platform

EcoIQ uses AI to compile, score, and publish climate intelligence profiles for industrial companies. Each profile scores six pillars (Public Benefit, Environmental Stewardship, Responsible Modernization, Transparent Governance, Anti-Corruption, and Ethical Alignment) from publicly available data.

→No manual entry required — AI ingests sustainability reports, news, regulatory filings
→Publishable in hours — new company profiles can be added and scored in < 1 day
→Defensible methodology — 6-pillar weighted scoring framework with public transparency
→Revenue from day one — companies pay £500–£50,000/mo to claim, verify, and improve profiles

Market Opportunity

ESG Data Market

The global ESG data and ratings market is projected to reach $2.1B by 2026 (Bloomberg Intelligence). Mid-market and frontier market companies represent the fastest-growing underserved segment.

Ethical Finance

The values-aligned and ethical finance sector manages £4T+ in global assets — with institutional mandates increasingly requiring ESG-screened portfolios. EcoIQ's scoring framework is uniquely aligned with these due-diligence requirements.

Climate Transition Finance

Development banks (EBRD, ADB, IFC) and sovereign wealth funds have committed $100B+ to emerging market climate transitions — all requiring company-level ESG data that currently doesn't exist at scale.

Central Asia + Gulf Focus
