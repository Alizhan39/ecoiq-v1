◈ Ethical Finance Architecture

The Rizq Distribution Model

EcoIQ is structured around the principle that value — rizq — should be distributed equitably across all those who contribute to or are affected by economic activity. This model underpins our scoring weights, capital compatibility assessments, and long-term stewardship framework.

01

Equitable Capital Deployment

Capital should flow to companies that distribute economic returns broadly — to employees, communities, and long-term reinvestment — not concentrated extraction by shareholders alone.

02

Long-Term Stewardship

Responsible finance prioritises durable value creation over short-term profit extraction. EcoIQ's Future Readiness pillar scores companies on their long-horizon orientation and climate transition investment.

03

Public Benefit Accountability

Every company is scored on its contribution to public welfare — job creation, community investment, social infrastructure, and environmental restoration — not just financial performance.

04

Transparent Governance

Rizq flows freely only where governance is transparent. EcoIQ scores board independence, anti-corruption safeguards, disclosure quality, and accountability mechanisms as core indicators.

05

Harm-Adjusted Returns

Companies that generate profits by externalising environmental or social costs receive a harm penalty of up to –30 points. True rizq cannot be built on harm to others.

06

Ethical Finance Compatibility

The model is designed to be compatible with responsible capital frameworks that emphasise stewardship, prohibition of exploitative structures, and alignment with broader human welfare — regardless of the specific framework tradition.

The Rizq Distribution Model is a conceptual framework developed by EcoIQ to structure ethical scoring weights and capital compatibility assessments. It is not a Shariah compliance opinion, religious ruling, or regulatory determination. All outputs are AI-assisted and indicative.
