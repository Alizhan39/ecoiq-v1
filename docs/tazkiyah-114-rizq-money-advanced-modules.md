# Tazkiyah 114 — Rizq & Money Advanced Modules

### Modern financial anxiety · halal business · family pressure · status spending · barakah-centred growth

> **Status:** Product concept (documentation). Pending sources, tafsir references, qualified scholar
> review, and wellbeing review (financial distress is sensitive) before any public use.
>
> **Not financial advice, not a fatwa, not a zakat/riba ruling, not a replacement for qualified
> scholars or financial professionals.** Everything is **reflection inspired by Qur'anic themes**.
> Builds on [Money & Rizq Pathway](tazkiyah-114-money-rizq-pathway.md) and the
> [Deep System](tazkiyah-114-money-rizq-deep-system.md). Documentation only — no routes, navigation,
> models, or UI.

---

## Safety & boundaries (binding)

- **Not financial advice. Not a fatwa. Not a zakat calculator. Not a riba ruling.**
- **No prosperity gospel.** Never imply worship guarantees wealth, or that giving "returns" money.
- **No shaming poverty. No glorifying wealth.** Worth before Allah is never a balance.
- **Serious debt / legal / financial crisis requires a professional** financial adviser; distress
  requires professional wellbeing support.
- **Religious rulings (riba, zakat, contracts) require qualified scholars** — always route out.
- Framing is always *"reflection inspired by Qur'anic themes."* Every module carries a
  content-status label; sensitive modules require wellbeing review.

## The fifteen advanced modules

Each is a *lens* onto the [Repair Engine](tazkiyah-114-repair-engine.md): a money pain → a Qur'anic
reflection → one small action → a dua prompt → optional tracking. Phase tags: **MVP** or **Later**.

### 1. Rizq Emergency Mode — *(MVP · sensitive)* · Fear
For urgent money panic — rent, debt, temptation toward haram income, shame, fear. A calm structure
to prevent panic decisions.
- **Flow:** Qur'anic reminder → reality check → one halal action → seek support → dua prompt → *avoid panic decisions*.
- **Safety:** prominent route to professional financial + wellbeing help; never pushes a haram "solution".

### 2. Halal Opportunity Filter — *(MVP)* · Halal
A **non-fatwa** reflection checklist for jobs, clients, partnerships, investments, business deals.
- **Questions:** Is the product/service halal? Any deception? Any exploitation? Any riba? Is the contract clear? Who could be harmed? What are the red flags?
- **Safety:** prompts only; doubtful contracts → qualified scholar.

### 3. Growth Without Losing Barakah — *(MVP)* · Barakah / Growth
A founder/business reflection: is growth costing your salah, family, workers, ethics, or humility?
- **Flow:** name what growth is pressuring → one boundary to protect it → reflect on barakah over scale.

### 4. Money Triggers — *(MVP)* · Fear
Identify emotional triggers around money and move from fear/nafs reaction to responsible Qur'anic action.
- **Flow:** spot the trigger → the pause → the chosen response → dua.

### 5. Family Rizq Council — *(Later)* · Family
A family-mode weekly money reflection (done together, gently).
- **Prompts:** shukr · spending · waste · helping others · planning · dua.

### 6. Business Before Allah — *(Later)* · Justice
Monthly entrepreneur accountability (muhasabah for business).
- **Prompts:** marketing honesty · fair pay · customer dignity · kept promises · hidden terms · ego.

### 7. Anti-Exploitation Builder — *(Later)* · Justice
A business-ethics layer that asks *who has less power and who may be harmed silently.*
- **Flow:** map the people affected → who is most vulnerable → one protection/fairness action.

### 8. Rizq and Migration — *(Later · sensitive)* · Growth / Fear
For Muslims abroad — rent pressure, family support, status pressure, starting from zero, supporting
relatives, comparison.
- **Flow:** name the pressure → dignity + tawakkul reflection → one sustainable step → boundary for over-support.
- **Safety:** sensitive (vulnerability, isolation); route to community/professional support.

### 9. Luxury Detox — *(MVP)* · Barakah
A 7-day status-spending and comparison reset.
- **Flow:** mute one comparison source → pause one status purchase → gratitude for "enough" → give quietly → reflect.

### 10. Rizq and Women — *(Later · sensitive)* · Family
Financial dignity, dependence, divorce fear, motherhood, fair pay, overgiving, safety, and
boundaries.
- **Flow:** name the concern → worth-and-rights-before-Allah reflection → one dignified step → boundary/safety note.
- **Safety:** never counsels staying in financial abuse; boundaries are allowed; route harm to support services.

### 11. When Helping Family Hurts — *(Later · sensitive)* · Family
Generosity *with boundaries* — supporting family without enabling harm.
- **Flow:** name the dynamic → what is owed vs what enables → a compassionate boundary → dua.
- **Safety:** distinguishes obligation from being exploited; never frames boundaries as sin.

### 12. Sadaqah Strategy — *(MVP)* · Growth
Intentional giving categories and sincerity checks.
- **Flow:** choose a giving intention → give quietly → riya-vs-ikhlas self-check → protect the receiver's dignity.

### 13. No-Riba Future Ideas — *(Later · concept-only)* · Halal
**Future-only educational concepts** — e.g. halal savings *education*, risk-sharing finance
*education*, an ethical-investment *reflection* checklist.
- ⚠️ **Strong warning:** **no financial products, recommendations, or services** without legal,
  regulatory, and scholarly review. This module is education/reflection only and ships last, if at all.

### 14. Rizq and Mental Load — *(MVP · sensitive)* · Fear
How financial stress affects character, family, salah, sleep, anger, and shame.
- **Flow:** name where the stress is leaking → one act of self-care/repair → dua → route to support.
- **Safety:** not therapy; chronic stress/anxiety → professional help.

### 15. The Rizq Map — *(MVP)* · Fear
A reflection that rizq can come through **many doors**, not one: skills, work, trade, family,
opportunity, health, time, knowledge, relationships, sadaqah, and unexpected openings.
- **Flow:** see the many doors → notice doors you've overlooked → one step toward a lawful door → trust the Opener (Al-Fattah).

## Sample user journeys

**A · Rizq emergency (sensitive, MVP)**
```
"I can't make rent and I'm tempted by haram income"
  → Rizq Emergency Mode: Qur'anic reminder (a way out for the mindful)
  → reality check (what is true right now?) → one halal action (a call, a request, a plan)
  → SEEK SUPPORT: "for serious debt, seek qualified financial advice; if in distress,
     wellbeing support" → dua prompt → DO NOT make a panic/haram decision tonight
```

**B · Founder checking barakah**
```
Growth Without Losing Barakah → "is scale costing my salah/family/ethics?"
  → one boundary (protect Fajr / a family evening / fair pay)
  → Business Before Allah monthly check → reflect on barakah over numbers
```

**C · Helping family without harm (sensitive)**
```
When Helping Family Hurts → name the dynamic → what is owed vs what enables
  → a compassionate boundary → dua → (if harm/abuse) route to support services
```

## Scholar-review requirements

- No module publishes without: **cited Qur'an translation source → tafsir references →
  scholar review → publishable**
  (per the [review workflow](tazkiyah-114-transformation-operating-system.md#e-scholar-review-layer)).
- **Names of Allah** (e.g. Al-Fattah, Ar-Razzaq) require scholar review and cited sources.
- **Halal Opportunity Filter, No-Riba Future Ideas** require explicit scholar sign-off that they
  contain **no rulings** and route correctly to qualified guidance.
- **No-Riba Future Ideas** additionally requires **legal + regulatory** review before any
  product/service language exists; until then it is education/reflection only.
- Any change to an ayah, translation, source, or sensitive wording **reopens review**.

## Wellbeing & financial-crisis safeguards

- Sensitive modules (Rizq Emergency Mode, Rizq & Mental Load, Rizq & Migration, Rizq & Women, When
  Helping Family Hurts) require **scholar + wellbeing reviewer** before publish.
- A persistent, visible route: *"For serious debt, legal, or financial crisis, seek qualified
  financial advice. If you feel unsafe or in distress, seek professional wellbeing support."*
- Never use urgency, guilt, or prosperity-gospel framing; never imply worship yields money.
- Family/women modules must show boundaries are allowed and **never** counsel staying in financial
  abuse; route harm to support services.
