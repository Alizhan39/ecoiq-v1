# Tazkiyah 114 — Money & Rizq Deep System

### A Qur'anic financial transformation system

> **Status:** Product concept (documentation). Pending sources, tafsir references, qualified
> scholar review, and wellbeing review (financial distress is sensitive) before any public use.
>
> **This is not financial advice, not a fatwa, not a zakat/riba ruling, and not a replacement for
> qualified scholars or financial professionals.** Everything is **reflection inspired by Qur'anic
> themes**. Builds on [Money & Rizq Pathway](tazkiyah-114-money-rizq-pathway.md).

---

## Safety & boundaries (binding)

- **Not financial advice.** No investment, budgeting, or debt guidance presented as advice.
- **Not a fatwa. Not a zakat calculator. Not a riba ruling.** Religious/legal rulings (riba,
  zakat, contracts, inheritance) require **qualified scholarly guidance** — always route out.
- **Serious debt / legal / financial crisis requires a professional** financial adviser and, where
  there is distress, professional wellbeing support.
- **No prosperity gospel.** Never imply worship "buys" wealth, or that giving guarantees a return.
- **No shaming poverty. No glorifying wealth.** Worth before Allah is never a balance.
- Framing is always *"reflection inspired by Qur'anic themes"* — never authoritative tafsir.
- Every module ships with a content-status label and a `scholar_review_status`; sensitive modules
  also require wellbeing review.

## The twenty modules

Each module is a *lens* onto the [Repair Engine](tazkiyah-114-repair-engine.md): a money pain → a
Qur'anic reflection → one small action → a dua prompt → optional tracking. Phase tags: **MVP** or
**Later**.

### 1. Rizq vs Control — *(MVP)*
Reflection for money anxiety and the addiction to control. Names the difference between *prudence*
(effort + planning) and *control* (the illusion that outcomes are ours).
- **Experience:** name the fear → "what is mine to do vs Allah's to decide?" → one halal action → release.
- **Safety:** reflection, not therapy; chronic anxiety → professional support.

### 2. Money and Identity Repair — *(MVP)*
Repairs shame and pride tied to income, wealth, postcode, lifestyle, or business success.
- **Experience:** name the identity hook → reflect on worth-before-Allah → one act that decouples self from status.
- **Safety:** sensitive (self-worth); avoid both shaming and glorifying.

### 3. Halal Ambition — *(MVP)*
Distinguishes healthy ambition (sincere striving for good) from ego-driven ambition (status, rivalry).
- **Experience:** examine the *why* behind a goal → reframe intention → keep the goal, purify the motive.

### 4. Business Niyyah Builder — *(MVP)*
A guided intention tool for founders, workers, sellers, and professionals — set niyyah before work.
- **Experience:** choose your role → guided prompts (service, honesty, benefit, Allah's pleasure) → write your own niyyah.

### 5. Riba Pressure Pathway — *(Later · sensitive)*
A **non-fatwa** reflection pathway for people feeling trapped by riba-based systems.
- **Experience:** name the pressure → reflect on tawakkul + seeking lawful means → **route to a qualified scholar** for any ruling.
- **Safety:** never rules on riba; explicit scholar referral; never shames the person.

### 6. Debt Without Despair — *(MVP · sensitive)*
A practical and spiritual pathway for debt anxiety, responsibility, repayment dignity.
- **Experience:** name the debt honestly → one repayment/communication step → dua for relief → companionship, not panic.
- **Safety:** route to qualified financial help; for distress, wellbeing support.

### 7. Before You Buy — *(MVP)*
A spending reflection for emotional spending, comparison, and status purchases.
- **Experience:** pause prompt before a purchase → "need / want / status / emotion?" → wait or proceed with intention.

### 8. Israf Detector — *(MVP)*
A waste and overconsumption reflection.
- **Experience:** spot one source of israf this week → reduce it → notice the barakah of "enough".

### 9. Fair Pay & Worker Amanah — *(Later)*
Business ethics for employers and managers.
- **Experience:** checklist prompts — paying on time and in full, fair wages, dignity, no exploitation.
- **Safety:** reflection prompts, not employment-law advice.

### 10. Customer Honesty Checklist — *(Later)*
Sales and marketing ethics.
- **Experience:** prompts — no lying, no exaggeration, no hidden defects, honest claims, no manipulation.

### 11. Money and Family Peace — *(Later · sensitive)*
For family conflict, resentment, secrecy, support, and money boundaries.
- **Experience:** name the tension → what is owed / what boundary is allowed → one repair action → dua.
- **Safety:** boundaries are allowed; never counsels staying in financial abuse; route harm to support.

### 12. Secret Giving — *(MVP)*
A sadaqah sincerity and dignity module.
- **Experience:** give small and quietly → reflect on riya vs ikhlas → protect the dignity of the receiver.

### 13. Zakat Readiness — *(Later)*
**Not a calculator.** A preparation checklist of records to gather *before* seeking qualified guidance.
- **Experience:** gather assets/records → understand the categories at a high level → **see a scholar/qualified zakat service** to calculate.
- **Safety:** explicitly defers all calculation and rulings to scholars.

### 14. Wealth Fitnah Detector — *(MVP)*
Reflection for arrogance, showing off, neglect of worship, and harshness as wealth increases.
- **Experience:** self-check questions → one humbling action (give quietly, serve, soften) → dua.

### 15. Poverty Shame Repair — *(MVP · sensitive)*
Reflection for dignity, sabr, and not tying worth to income.
- **Experience:** name the shame → worth-before-Allah reflection → one dignified next step → dua.
- **Safety:** never shames; routes hardship to practical support.

### 16. Rizq Through Skills — *(MVP)*
A halal earning growth plan: dua + skill + service + consistency + means.
- **Experience:** pick one lawful skill → one small step this week → effort-as-worship framing.

### 17. Monthly Money Mizan — *(MVP)*
A monthly accountability reflection dashboard: **income · spending · giving · debt · family · heart · next action.**
- **Experience:** answer seven gentle prompts → one correction for next month → private journal.

### 18. Rizq Names of Allah Pathway — *(MVP)*
Connects money fears to Names of Allah: **Ar-Razzaq** (Provider), **Al-Fattah** (Opener),
**Al-Latif** (Subtle/Kind), **Al-Wakil** (Trustee), **Al-Ghani** (Self-Sufficient), **Al-Mughni**
(Enricher).
- **Experience:** match the fear to a Name → reflection + dua prompt → carry the Name for the day.
- **Safety:** Names/meanings need scholar review and cited sources.

### 19. Three Money States — *(MVP)*
A diagnostic lens: **fear-driven money · ego-driven money · amanah-driven money.**
- **Experience:** identify your current state → the shift toward amanah → one action that moves you there.

### 20. Wealth as Test Simulator — *(Later)*
Interactive ethical scenarios for money choices (e.g. a lucrative-but-doubtful deal, an unfair
advantage, a chance to give quietly).
- **Experience:** choose a response → see the values in tension (honesty, amanah, tawakkul) → reflect.
- **Safety:** scenarios model *reflection*, never rulings; outcomes are not "scored" as right/wrong fatwa.

## Sample user flows

**A · Rizq anxiety (MVP spine)**
```
Struggle "I'm scared about money"
  → Rizq vs Control: what is mine to do vs Allah's to decide?
  → Rizq Names pathway: carry Ar-Razzaq today
  → one halal action (a CV, a call, a budget line)
  → small secret sadaqah while scared
  → dua prompt → journal one line → trust the outcome
```

**B · About to make a status purchase**
```
Before You Buy pause → "need / want / status / emotion?"
  → Israf Detector reflection → wait 24 hours or proceed with intention
  → journal what the urge was really about
```

**C · Debt anxiety (sensitive)**
```
Debt Without Despair → name the debt honestly → one repayment/communication step
  → dua for relief → CLEAR ROUTE: "for serious debt, seek qualified financial advice;
     for distress, wellbeing support" → companionship, not panic
```

## Example cards

**Card — Three Money States**
> **Where is your money coming from today?**
> *Fear-driven* (panic, control, scarcity) · *Ego-driven* (status, rivalry, showing off) ·
> *Amanah-driven* (trust, service, responsibility).
> One step toward amanah: give something small, quietly, today.
> *Reflection inspired by Qur'anic themes · pending scholar review.*

**Card — Rizq Names pathway (Ar-Razzaq)**
> **Fear: "There won't be enough."**
> Turn to **Ar-Razzaq** — the Provider. Provision is His to apportion and guarantee; your part is
> honest effort. *Dua prompt (your words):* "O Allah, Ar-Razzaq, suffice me with the halal, and
> free my heart from the fear of want."
> *Name and meaning pending scholar review + cited source.*

**Card — Before You Buy**
> **Pause before you buy.** Is this a need, a want, a status purchase, or an emotion?
> If it's emotion or status, wait 24 hours. If it's a need, buy it with gratitude.
> *Reflection, not financial advice.*

## Scholar-review requirements

- No module publishes without: **cited Qur'an translation source → tafsir references →
  scholar review → publishable** (per the
  [review workflow](tazkiyah-114-transformation-operating-system.md#e-scholar-review-layer)).
- **Names of Allah** (meanings, application) require scholar review and cited sources.
- **Riba Pressure, Zakat Readiness** require explicit scholar sign-off that they contain **no
  rulings** and route correctly to qualified guidance.
- Any change to an ayah, translation, source, or sensitive wording **reopens review**.

## Wellbeing / financial-crisis safeguards

- Sensitive modules (Debt Without Despair, Poverty Shame Repair, Money & Family Peace, Riba
  Pressure, Money & Identity Repair) require **scholar + wellbeing reviewer** before publish.
- A persistent, visible route: *"For serious debt, legal, or financial crisis, seek qualified
  financial advice. If you feel unsafe or in distress, seek professional wellbeing support."*
- Never use urgency, guilt, or prosperity-gospel framing. Never imply worship yields money.
- Money & Family Peace must show boundaries are allowed and **never** counsel staying in financial
  abuse; route harm to support services.
