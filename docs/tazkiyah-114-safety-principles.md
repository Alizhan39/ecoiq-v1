# Tazkiyah 114 — Safety & Trust Principles

> These principles are binding on all Tazkiyah 114 content and product decisions. They exist to
> protect users and to keep the platform humble. Where a principle and a feature conflict, the
> principle wins.

---

## 1. What this platform is not

This platform is **not** a replacement for:

- **Scholars** — for religious or legal rulings (fatwa), seek qualified scholarly guidance.
- **Therapists / mental-health professionals** — for emotional or psychological distress.
- **Doctors** — for any medical or health concern.
- **Emergency services** — for crisis, danger, or risk of harm.
- **Family / legal protection** — for safety, abuse, or legal matters.

Tazkiyah 114 offers **Qur'anic reflection and personal growth** — companionship and small steps,
not authority over your deen, your health, or your safety.

---

## 2. Character, patience, and harm

- **Qur'anic character does not mean accepting abuse.** Patience, mercy, and good character are
  never a reason to remain in harm.
- **Sabr does not mean silence against injustice.** Seeking justice, speaking truth, and
  protecting the wronged are part of the deen, not a failure of patience.
- **Forgiveness does not always mean returning to harm.** A person can forgive in their heart and
  still keep a boundary, distance, or seek protection.
- Every relationship/boundary/abuse-adjacent pathway must show a **boundaries + safety block** and
  route to qualified counsel and, where there is danger, to protection services.

---

## 3. Worship and reflection integrity

- **Istikhara is not reduced to dreams.** It is a prayer for guidance; outcomes unfold through
  circumstances and sound counsel, not dreams alone.
- **Du'a prompts are not authentic narrated du'as** unless explicitly sourced and labelled.
  Generated lines are *prompts* to help a person speak to Allah in their own words; narrated
  (Sunnah) du'as are presented separately, with their source.
- **Any tafsir, translation, hadith, or fiqh-based guidance needs review** before publishing.
  Until then, content is framed as *reflection inspired by Qur'anic themes*, never as authoritative
  meaning.

---

## 4. Emotional safety

- **Sensitive topics require additional wellbeing review** (a scholar **and** a wellbeing
  reviewer): sin/addiction/despair, grief, abuse-adjacent relationships, money desperation,
  body/health, self-worth collapse, and community spaces.
- **Avoid spiritual shame.** Never imply a person's suffering is punishment, or that struggling
  means weak faith.
- **Avoid toxic positivity.** Do not paper over real pain with slogans; hold both honesty and hope.
- **Avoid simplistic answers to trauma.** Trauma and grief are not "fixed" by a verse and an
  action; offer companionship and route to professional support.
- Streaks and trackers count *practice done*, never *app opened*; missed days are never shamed.

---

## 5. Anti-addiction, anti-vanity design

- **No vanity metrics.**
- **No public ranking of worship.**
- **No follower-based influencer culture** (no follower counts by default).
- **No infinite scroll by default.**
- Community is small, bounded, and purpose-led; identity and visibility are opt-in; privacy and
  anonymity are the default.

---

## 6. Standard wording

Use language that stays humble and accurate:

- "Qur'anic reflection"
- "inspired by Qur'anic themes"
- "seek qualified scholarly guidance for religious/legal rulings"
- "seek professional help in a mental-health crisis"
- "content pending scholar review"

Avoid: "this surah means exactly…", "the ruling is…", "this will heal/fix…", or any predictive,
mystical, or fortune-telling framing.

---

## 7. Content status labels

| Label | Meaning |
|---|---|
| `author_draft` | Authored; not yet sourced or reviewed. |
| `source_added` | Translation/tafsir references attached; awaiting scholar. |
| `needs_scholar_review` | In the scholar queue. |
| `scholar_reviewed` | Approved by a qualified reviewer. |
| `publishable` | Reviewed and cleared to publish. |
| `sensitive_review_required` | Requires scholar **and** wellbeing review before publishing. |

**Any change** to an ayah, translation, source, or sensitive wording **reopens review.**

---

## 8. The permanent trust note

Shown on every page:

> "This platform is for Qur'anic reflection and personal growth. It does not replace qualified
> scholars, tafsir study, therapy, medical support, legal advice, or fatwa. Where interpretation
> is provided, it should be reviewed by qualified Islamic knowledge holders."

And, on reflection/crisis tools:

> "This is not a fatwa, not therapy, and not a diagnosis. It is a guided Qur'anic reflection tool."
