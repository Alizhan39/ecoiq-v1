EcoIQ Justice & Maqasid Intelligence Layer

Ethical AI agents for just, sustainable, and future-ready enterprise modernisation.

This is a hackathon MVP and a conceptual governance layer, not a certified ethics or compliance product. It is presented as an ethical governance, justice, stakeholder-impact, and long-term stewardship lens — inspired by Maqasid al-Shariah, amanah, khalifa stewardship, and intergenerational justice — for professional, inclusive, non-sectarian use in enterprise modernisation decisions.

1. Why This Layer Exists

Most enterprise AI tools optimise for speed, cost, and efficiency. EcoIQ adds a justice and stewardship layer so that modernisation plans are evaluated for:

  • Human impact
  • Worker transition
  • Community protection
  • Environmental stewardship
  • Fair allocation of costs and benefits
  • Future generations
  • Governance and accountability

2. Maqasid-Inspired Evaluation Framework

Five classical protective principles (Maqasid al-Shariah), plus amanah / khalifa stewardship of the Earth, reframed as modernisation-planning questions.

PrincipleMeaningEcoIQ modernisation questionExample riskExample mitigation
Hifz al-Nafs Protection of life Does this plan reduce harm to people's health, safety, and living conditions? Pollution, unsafe infrastructure, energy poverty, heating failure Prioritise safety, clean heat, reliable infrastructure, vulnerable households
Hifz al-Aql Protection of intellect Does this plan improve access to knowledge, transparency, and informed decision-making? Opaque AI decisions, hidden assumptions, poor public understanding Evidence-first reports, audit logs, explainable recommendations
Hifz al-Mal Protection of wealth and resources Does this plan protect public money, investor capital, and household affordability? Wasteful capex, corruption, stranded assets, unfair tariffs Transparent cost-benefit analysis, phased investment, audit trail
Hifz al-Nasl Protection of family / community / future generations Does this plan protect families, communities, and future generations? Job losses, regional inequality, unaffordable transition, climate burden passed to children Worker retraining, community transition funds, long-term climate resilience
Hifz al-Din / Values Protection of values and moral responsibility Does this plan align with trust, accountability, justice, and responsibility? Greenwashing, extractive investment, hidden harm Ethical governance, transparent evidence, stakeholder accountability
Amanah / Khalifa Stewardship Protection of Earth Does this plan treat natural resources as a trust rather than something to exploit? Water waste, emissions, ecosystem damage, short-term profit over long-term harm Emissions reduction, circular economy, water protection, biodiversity safeguards

3. Justice-Aware Agent Workflow

A justice-aware pipeline sits alongside the Conduct-style legacy analysis already in this module.

  1. Modernisation Plan
  2. Permission Guard Agent
  3. Evidence Retrieval
  4. Conduct-style Legacy Analysis
  5. Maqasid Justice Review Agent
  6. Future Generations Impact Agent
  7. Worker & Community Risk Agent
  8. Final Just Transition Plan
  9. Audit Log

4. Justice & Stewardship Agents

Five conceptual agents extending the AI Workforce described on the Model + Enterprise Integration Readiness page. Conceptual in this hackathon build — not wired to a live scoring model.

Justice Review Agent

Input: modernisation plan, stakeholders, risks, evidence

Output: justice score, fairness risks, mitigation actions

Maqasid Impact Agent

Input: plan and evidence

Output: Maqasid impact matrix

Future Generations Agent

Input: long-term climate and infrastructure risks

Output: intergenerational impact warning

Worker & Community Agent

Input: affected systems, sectors, regions, workforce risks

Output: just transition recommendations

Amanah Stewardship Agent

Input: environmental and resource impact

Output: stewardship recommendations

5. Demo Output

Question: "Create a modernisation plan for Samruk Energy."

Normal enterprise AI answer

"Replace legacy coal boilers, optimise grid upgrades, and phase capex."

EcoIQ Justice & Maqasid-enhanced answer

"Replace legacy coal boilers through a phased clean-heat transition, but include worker retraining, household affordability protection, transparent capex governance, public health prioritisation, and long-term emissions reduction. The plan should not transfer costs unfairly to vulnerable households or future generations."

Illustrative example, not a live model output.
